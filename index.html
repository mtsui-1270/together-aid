The Issues

Four crises, one system.

Global healthcare is failing billions of people across four interconnected dimensions. Understanding them is the first step to solving them.

01

Insurance Coverage Gaps

Hundreds of millions of people have no health insurance at all. Without coverage, a single illness can mean financial ruin — or going without care entirely.

↑ Growing in low-income regions
02

Healthcare Affordability

Even when care exists, people in low-income countries pay a disproportionate share out of their own pocket — pushing families into poverty to stay alive.

↑ Up to 40% OOP in some regions
03

Quality Disparities

Access to care is meaningless without quality. Healthcare quality scores vary dramatically between high and low-income nations, creating a two-tier global system.

↑ 3× gap between richest and poorest
04

Worker Shortages

The world faces a critical shortage of doctors, nurses, and health workers — especially in sub-Saharan Africa and South Asia where the need is greatest.

↑ 55M+ workers needed by 2030

Healthcare Quality Around the World
The Universal Health Coverage index measures how well each country delivers essential health services to its population. Darker blue = better coverage.
Source: Our World in Data · WHO UHC Index
Who Pays the Most Out of Pocket?
Countries with lower GDP per capita force their citizens to pay a far higher share of healthcare costs themselves — the people least able to afford it bear the greatest burden.
Source: WHO Global Health Expenditure Database
Government Health Spending by Region
How much governments invest in healthcare reveals who is protected by public systems — and who is left to fend for themselves.

What You Can Do

Access. Resources. Action.

Information is the first step. Here are real, affordable options for people navigating the global healthcare system.

For Uninsured Individuals

  • Community Health Centers
    Federally funded clinics that provide care on a sliding-scale fee based on income — regardless of insurance status.
    Find a center near you →
  • Medicaid & CHIP
    US government programs offering free or low-cost health coverage to eligible low-income adults, children, and families.
    Check eligibility →
  • HealthCare.gov Marketplace
    Compare and enroll in health insurance plans, with subsidies available based on income level.
    Browse plans →

About This Project

Built to inform.
Designed to act.

✦ Kode With Klossy · Data Science Camp

Together.aid was created as part of the Kode With Klossy Data Science program — a camp dedicated to empowering young women in tech and data.

This project uses real WHO and global health data to visualize healthcare inequalities and connect people with resources. Every chart is built from scratch using Python and real-world datasets.

The goal is simple: more people should understand these issues, and more people should know help exists.
